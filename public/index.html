I'm Jeffrey Wang from Brooklyn.

Programming always seemed elusive and mysterious to me. As a data manager I was envious of the programmers on the job, with their ability to fix things on the fly and their seemingly boundless knowledge on how our systems worked.

At H2O Clinical, I developed electronic case report forms (eCRFs) while monitoring UX to make sure that our clinicians knew how to navigate and utilize our data capture system. I was given the responsibility to train users on how to use our system. Through training other people, this incited a passion in me that I knew I wanted to explore: becoming a teacher.

I was accepted into the NYC Teaching Fellows program in 2014 where I taught math in a middle school in East New York, and then found myself teaching computer science in a charter school in Park Slope, where I taught students how to code and my curiosity for programming was revitalized.
